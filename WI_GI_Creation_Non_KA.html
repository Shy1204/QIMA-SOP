Work Instruction — Level 3

GI Creation — Non-KA Cases

Phase 1 — Order Preparation with Client Technical Team CSC Team Support Team
Where this fits in the Audit Execution SOP (Non-KA)
This Work Instruction is a sub-process of the Audit Execution SOP Non-KA and sits within Phase 1 — Order Preparation with Client.

It is triggered when a client places an order with remarks indicating a GI requirement and no valid GI currently exists in SSO for that client — or when an existing GI needs to be updated following new client instructions.

The output of this WI — an approved GI uploaded and synced in SSO — is a prerequisite for all subsequent audit execution phases. Audits cannot proceed correctly without an active and current GI on the order.
Overview — what this process covers
A General Instruction (GI) defines how a specific client's audits must be conducted — covering reporting format, scoring approach, protocol rules, coordination requirements, and any client-specific standards. Each GI is client-specific and product-category-specific.

This WI covers the full lifecycle from intake to GI active in the SSO master list:
  1. Check if a GI already exists in SSO → Section 1
  2. Clarify requirements and identify the GI type → Section 2
  3. Draft the GI (Technical Team or CSC Team, depending on type) → Section 3
  4. Validate the draft against client request and audit standards → Section 4
  5. Submit to manager for review and approval → Section 5
  6. Save as PDF and apply the correct file naming convention → Section 6
  7. Send to Support Team for SSO upload → Section 7
  8. Support Team: update the GI in SSO → Section 8
  9. Sync GI to pending orders and master list → Section 9
  10. Notify the audit team → Section 10
1
Check if a GI already exists in SSO
1
Open the client account in SSO
Search for the client by name in SSO. Open the client account and navigate to the General Instructions (GI) section of the account profile.
2
Review the client's GI master list
Check all GIs currently listed under this client account. Confirm whether a GI already exists that covers the relevant audit type and product category for the current order.
3
Decision — GI exists or not?
GI exists and is current → Use the existing GI in SSO. No new GI needs to be created. Proceed with the order normally.

No GI exists, or existing GI needs updating → Proceed to Section 2 to clarify requirements and begin the creation process.
2
Clarify requirements and identify the GI type
1
Read the order remarks carefully
Open the order and read all client remarks in full. The remarks typically specify: the product category, the GI name to use, the audit type, scoring instructions, and any special protocol the client has provided. Do not skim — missed details in remarks are a leading cause of incorrect GIs.
2
If requirements are unclear — clarify with Sales or the client before drafting
If the remarks are incomplete or ambiguous, Sales and CSC must contact the client to confirm scope before any drafting begins. Do not make assumptions. A GI drafted on incorrect assumptions will require a full revision cycle and may delay the audit.
Image 1 — Example client email providing GI details
3
Identify the audit type and determine which GI type is needed
Based on the client requirements, determine which GI type(s) are needed. This determines who drafts the GI in Section 3.
GI type Who drafts Typical content covered
Report Technical Team Reporting format, scoring method, colour rating thresholds, CAP requirements, presentation layout
Protocol Technical Team Audit checklist, critical NC definitions, sampling plan, product-specific test requirements
Coordination CSC Team Announcement rules, factory contact rules, booking window, language requirements
Support / Booking CSC Team Order creation rules, booking instructions, client portal upload requirements
3
Draft the GI
If GI type is Report or Protocol → Technical Team drafts
1
Collect all source documents before starting
Gather: the client requirements email, any previous GI versions, the applicable audit protocol, and any scoring or colour rating instructions specified by the client. Do not start drafting without having all source materials in hand.
2
Draft the GI following the client's instructions precisely
Write the GI content based on what the client has specified. Pay particular attention to scoring thresholds, colour rating definitions, critical NC criteria, and reporting format — clients often provide these explicitly and they must be captured exactly.

If GI type is Coordination or Support → CSC Team drafts
1
Collect the client's coordination and booking requirements
Gather: the client requirements email, any previous coordination GI, booking rules, and applicable announcement instructions for this client.
2
Draft the GI covering all coordination and booking specifics
Write the GI covering: announcement type (announced / semi-announced / unannounced), factory contact rules, booking window, language preference, and any client-specific booking portal or order creation instructions.
Both GI types may be required at the same time
For some clients, a Technical GI (Report and/or Protocol) and a Coordination/Booking GI are needed simultaneously. In this case, each team drafts their respective GI in parallel. Both must reach approval before either is sent for SSO upload.
4
Validate the draft against client request and audit standards
1
Cross-check every item in the draft against the client's requirements
Go through the client's original email or requirements document line by line. Confirm that every instruction has been captured — including GI names per product category, scoring thresholds, reporting format, and any special instructions.
2
Verify the draft aligns with QIMA's audit standards
Ensure the GI content does not contradict QIMA's standard audit procedures. If any part of the client's requirements conflicts with QIMA's standards, flag this to the manager before proceeding — do not silently draft around a known conflict.
3
Decision — is the client request valid and applicable?
Yes, all requirements are clear and aligned → Proceed to Section 5 to submit for manager review.

No — something is unclear or conflicting → Flag the issue to the manager immediately. Clarify with the client before proceeding. Do not submit a draft with unresolved issues.
5
Submit draft GI to manager for review and approval
1
Send the draft GI to your manager via email or Teams
Attach the draft GI file. Include a brief note with: the client name, GI type(s) being submitted, and a mention of any points that required a judgment call during drafting so the manager can focus their review accordingly.
2
Manager reviews for accuracy, completeness, and client alignment
The manager (team leader) checks the GI against the client's requirements and QIMA's audit standards. Team leader sign-off is required before the GI proceeds to PDF formatting. This step is mandatory and cannot be skipped.
3
Decision — approved or revisions needed?
Approved → Proceed to Section 6 to save as PDF and apply the naming convention.

Revisions needed → Manager returns the draft with specific comments. Drafter addresses all comments and resubmits for review. Do not move to PDF until approval is confirmed in writing.
6
Save as PDF and apply the correct file naming convention
1
Export the approved GI as a PDF
Once manager approval is confirmed, save or export the GI as a PDF. Do not make any content changes after approval — the PDF must exactly reflect what was signed off.
2
Rename the PDF using the standard naming convention
Apply the following naming structure. Use the exact client code, product category, and date as specified in the client's requirements email.
File naming convention
{GI type} _ {Client code} _ {Product category} _ {DDMMYYYY}
Example filename GI type Notes
Technical Audit_CRG_Woven_Apparel_06042026 Protocol / Report CRG = Country Road Group · Date = 06 Apr 2026
Technical Audit_CRG_Knitwear_Apparel_06042026 Protocol / Report Separate file per product category
Technical Audit_CRG_Denim_Apparel_06042026 Protocol / Report Separate file per product category
Technical Audit CRG General Hardgoods 06042026 Silicone Products Protocol / Report Append product sub-type at end where needed
Important: The filename is how the Support Team and audit team identify this GI in SSO. Do not abbreviate, reorder, or change the format. Use the exact names provided by the client in their requirements email — not an internally invented shorthand.
7
Send the approved GI PDF to the Support Team
1
Compose an email to @Support
Address the email to the Support Team (tag @Support in the email or Teams message). Include: the client name, the GI type(s) being submitted, and a brief note of context — for example, whether this is a brand new GI or a replacement for an existing one.
2
Attach the correctly named PDF
Attach the renamed PDF file to the email. Double-check the filename before sending — the Support Team will use this exact name when creating the GI in SSO. An incorrect filename at this stage means it will need to be corrected in SSO later.
3
Send the email and retain a copy
Send the email. Keep a copy for your records. The Support Team will handle all SSO steps from this point — the Technical Team does not have GI uploading access in SSO.
Technical Team — no SSO GI upload access
The Technical Team cannot upload or modify GIs directly in SSO. All SSO actions — upload, sync, release, and notification — must be performed by the Support Team or CSC. Do not ask a colleague with SSO access to upload informally. All uploads must go through the correct Support Team channel to maintain the audit trail.
8
Support Team — update the GI in SSO
1
Open the client account in SSO and navigate to the GI section
Search for the client by name in SSO. Open the client account and go to the General Instructions section of the account profile.
2
Create a new GI version and attach the new PDF
Select the option to create a new version or add a new GI entry. Attach the PDF received from the Technical Team or CSC Team. Verify that the filename in SSO matches exactly what was provided.
3
Delete the old GI version
Remove the previous GI version. Only one active version should exist per GI type per client. Leaving old versions active creates ambiguity for auditors and coordinators pulling up the order.
4
Complete the SSO publishing flow: Save → Release → Confirm
Execute all three steps in sequence. Save the new GI, then Release it, then Confirm. All three steps are required for the GI to become active in SSO. Stopping after Save or Release will leave the GI in draft state and it will not appear on orders.
Verification: After confirming, return to the client's GI list in SSO and check that the new GI appears as active before proceeding to Section 9.
9
Sync the GI to pending orders and the master account list
1
Sync the new GI to all pending orders for this client
In SSO, use the sync function to apply the new GI to all current open and pending orders for this client. This ensures that auditors and coordinators working on existing orders will see the updated GI immediately, not just on new orders created after today.
2
Sync to the master account list in SSO
Update the client's master account list in SSO to reflect the new GI. The master list is the reference used for all future orders — it must always be kept current. Failure to update the master list means the new GI may not automatically apply to future orders.
Do not skip the sync step
If the GI is uploaded but not synced to pending orders, auditors will continue to reference the old GI (or no GI) on their active orders. This is a common source of audit execution errors that only surfaces at the time of the audit — after which it is too late to correct without rescheduling.
10
Notify the audit team of the new GI
1
Send a notification to all relevant teams
Once the GI is confirmed active and synced, notify: the audit back office, and any coordinators currently managing open orders for this client. The notification should include: client name, GI name(s) updated, effective date, and a brief summary of what changed (new GI vs. replacement, product categories affected).
2
Confirm the GI is visible in the SSO master list — process complete
Do a final check: open the SSO master list for this client and verify the new GI appears as active. This is the final confirmation step. The GI creation process is complete once the GI is active in the SSO master list and all relevant teams have been notified.
Process complete ✓
The GI is now active in SSO, synced to all pending orders, reflected in the master account list, and the audit team has been informed. The order can now proceed through the Audit Execution SOP.
