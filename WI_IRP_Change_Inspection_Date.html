Work Instruction — Level 3

IRP — Change Inspection Date & Postponement Reasons

Phase 2 — Order Booking & Announcement Phase 3 — Validation & Allocation Coordination Team
Where this fits in the Audit Execution SOP (Non-KA)
This Work Instruction is a sub-process of the Audit Execution SOP Non-KA and applies across two phases:

• Phase 2 — Order Booking & Announcement: when the factory cannot confirm the proposed date, or when the announcement triggers a postponement request from the factory or client.
• Phase 3 — Validation & Allocation: when allocation issues (auditor unavailability, travel, payment hold) require moving the inspection date before the audit proceeds.

It can also be triggered reactively at any point post-allocation when unforeseen events require rescheduling.
Overview — what this process is
When an inspection date needs to change — whether pushed earlier or postponed — the Coordination team must update it in IRP using the Change Inspection Date function. This ensures the system reflects the correct date, triggers the right notifications, and records the reason for audit trail purposes.

1
How to access Change Inspection Date in IRP
→
Open the order in IRP → click "Change Inspection Date" in the top menu
The button is in the action bar at the top of the order detail page, between "Contact Customer" and "Confirm Inspection Date".
IRP — "Change Inspection Date" dialog showing the full list of available postponement reasons
→
Fill in New Inspection Date, select a Reason, add Comments, then Confirm
The Comments field is mandatory for internal clarity — always describe the situation briefly. Then decide whether to keep "Send notification mail to client" ticked.
Example: Reason = "QIMA is not ready" with internal comment explaining that allocation is still being confirmed. Notification to client is unticked.
2
Postponement reasons — when to use each one
Reason Type When to use
Factory not ready — no date available PostponeFactory Factory has confirmed they are not ready but cannot yet give a new date. Use when rescheduling is pending factory availability.
Factory is not ready PostponeFactory Factory is not ready but a new date has been agreed. More specific than the above — use when the new date is known.
Cannot reach the factory PostponeFactory Coordination has been unable to contact the factory after repeated attempts. Use with a comment logging the contact attempts made.
Client has asked to postpone PostponeClient The client has explicitly requested a later date. Always include the client's name/email reference in Comments.
QIMA is not ready PostponeInternal Internal QIMA reason — auditor not yet allocated, allocation still being confirmed, or travel not yet arranged. Do not notify client (see section 3).
Factory is ready earlier EarlierFactory Factory has contacted us to move the inspection to an earlier date than originally planned.
Client has asked to inspect earlier EarlierClient Client has requested an earlier inspection date. Confirm auditor availability before changing.
Need to wait for approved samples PostponeClient Inspection cannot proceed until the client's approved samples arrive at the factory. Coordinate with client on expected sample arrival date.
Factory informed us that the Audit can be performed earlier EarlierFactory Factory proactively informed us they are ready before the scheduled date. Use when the factory (not the client) initiates the earlier date.
Bad weather conditions PostponeExternal Typhoon, flood, or severe weather prevents the auditor from reaching the factory or conducting the audit safely.
We are waiting for client further instructions before proceeding PostponeClient Inspection is on hold pending a decision or clarification from the client. Use when the client has not yet confirmed how to proceed.
Failed to receive formal confirmation from factory PostponeFactory Factory has not provided written confirmation of the inspection date within the required timeframe. Use this to log the delay formally.
Goods have been shipped PostponeFactory Goods have already left the factory and the inspection window has passed. Coordinate with client on whether a re-inspection is needed.
Waiting for client payment PostponeClient Order is on hold because payment has not been received. Coordinate with Finance/Support team before changing the date.
Goods are ready and QIMA can be ready earlier EarlierInternal Both the goods and the auditor are available earlier than planned. Use to bring forward the date proactively.
Missing Mandatory Documents required by client PostponeFactory Factory has not submitted required documents (e.g. tech packs, compliance certificates). Inspection cannot start until documents are received.
3
When to untick "Send notification mail to client"
Situation Notify client? Reason
QIMA is not ready — allocation still being confirmed Untick This is an internal admin change. The date shown is a placeholder while QIMA confirms the auditor. Notifying the client prematurely causes confusion.
Semi-announced or unannounced audit — date is being moved internally Untick If the client is also the audited factory (e.g. the supplier portal is linked), sending the notification exposes the real audit date and compromises the audit integrity.
Intermediate/working date — final date not yet confirmed Untick Only notify the client once the final confirmed date is set. Multiple notifications for interim changes create unnecessary noise.
Factory requested change and client already informed separately Untick Avoid sending a duplicate automated notification if the client was already contacted directly by email or phone.
Client-requested change (client asked to postpone / earlier) Keep ticked The client initiated the change — they should receive confirmation that the system has been updated.
Factory delay and client needs to be informed Keep ticked Client should be aware that the inspection has moved due to a factory issue so they can manage their own timelines.
Bad weather or external force majeure Keep ticked External events affecting inspection dates should always be communicated to the client transparently.
IRP system warning — unannounced / semi-announced audits
IRP displays this warning when you tick "Send notification mail to client": "If this is an Audit order, and the client is also the audited factory, enabling this option may expose the actual audit date to the factory, including for semi-announced or unannounced audits."

Always check the audit type before sending. When in doubt — untick and notify manually.
4
What happens after you click Confirm
1
IRP updates the Inspection Date on the order
The new date is immediately reflected in the Order Summary panel. The previous date and the reason for change are logged in the order history (click "Show History" to view).
2
System sends notification email to client (if ticked)
An automated email is sent to the client informing them of the new inspection date. This is triggered immediately on Confirm — it cannot be recalled.
3
Auditor App and auditor assignment reflect the new date
If an auditor has already been allocated, their assignment in the Auditor App is updated to the new date. Coordination should inform the auditor directly via Teams or phone to avoid any confusion.
4
Coordination must post a Teams message to the order group
Leave a message in the relevant Teams order thread noting: new date, reason selected, and whether the client was notified. This keeps the full team aligned.
Tip: If the change is due to an internal QIMA reason (auditor, allocation), always add a comment in the Comments field that says the new date is provisional and still being confirmed. This protects against the factory or client acting on an incorrect date.
